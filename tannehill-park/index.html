Park Attractions

From Civil War-era iron furnaces to miles of creek-side trails, there's something for everyone.

Historic

The Furnaces

Explore the towering remnants of 19th-century iron furnaces and coke ovens that powered Alabama's industrial revolution.
Events

Trade Days

Browse handcrafted goods, local art, and antiques at our beloved monthly marketplace and craft fair.
Nature

Trails & Creek

Hike through scenic woodland trails along Roupes Creek — perfect for families, birders, and nature lovers.
Stay

Camping & Cabins

Over 250 campsites from full hook-ups to primitive settings, plus cozy cabin rentals nestled in the woods.

Admission & Hours

The park is open every day of the year from sunrise to sunset. The Visitor Center and Museum are open 10am to 4pm daily.

Seniors (62+), military/veterans, and visitors with disabilities receive 15% off regular admission (excludes special event weekends).

Things to Do

Activities for Everyone

Iron & Steel Museum

Artifacts and exhibits documenting the region's rich industrial heritage.

Grist Mill

Tour a working grist mill and learn how grain was processed in the 1800s.

Blacksmithing

Watch live blacksmith demonstrations and iron pour events throughout the year.

Tannehill Creamery

Grab a sweet treat or a cold drink after a day of exploring the park.

Special Events

Civil War reenactments, craft fairs, car shows, races, and seasonal celebrations.

Miniature Railway

Take a ride on the miniature railway — a family favorite for all ages.
