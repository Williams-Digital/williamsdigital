About & History

The beautiful tree-lined valley, hillsides rich in ore, and swiftly flowing Roupes Creek made this a perfect setting.

History

From Iron Furnaces to State Park

The story of Tannehill spans nearly two centuries of Alabama history.

The Early Ironworks

In the early 1800s, the rich iron ore deposits and abundant timber along Roupes Creek attracted pioneers who established iron-making operations in this valley between what is now Birmingham and Tuscaloosa.

Civil War Production

During the Civil War, the Tannehill furnaces became a critical supplier of iron for the Confederate war effort, producing munitions, cookware, and other essential goods. The ironworks grew into one of Alabama's most important industrial sites.

Destruction & Decline

In March 1865, Union cavalry raiders destroyed the furnaces and supporting infrastructure, effectively ending iron production at Tannehill. The site fell into disuse and the forest slowly reclaimed the land.

Preservation & Rebirth

In the 1960s and 70s, a preservation movement led to the site being designated a state park. Volunteers and historians worked to restore structures, establish trails, and create the Iron and Steel Museum to tell the story of Alabama's iron-making heritage.

Tannehill Today

Now spanning 1,500 acres, Tannehill Ironworks Historical State Park welcomes visitors year-round for camping, hiking, historical tours, Trade Days, and special events — connecting the past to the present for generations to come.
